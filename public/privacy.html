InstaGRE Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

InstaGRE ("InstaGRE", "we", "us") is a GRE vocabulary study app available as an installable web app and on Google Play. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, who it is shared with, and how you can access, correct, or delete it.

Short version: InstaGRE stores your account (name, mobile number, date of birth) and your study progress (mastered words, tough-nut flags, streak, test history) so you can pick up where you left off across sessions. We don't show ads, sell your data, or share it with data brokers. If analytics is enabled, we use privacy-respecting, aggregated Google Analytics — never for advertising.

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you create an account, we collect:

DataWhy we collect it
Full nameDisplayed in your profile inside the app.
Mobile numberUsed as your account identifier / username to sign in.
Date of birthUsed as your account password. It is one-way hashed (bcrypt) before storage — we cannot read it back in plain text, and it is never used for age-based advertising or profiling.

Study progress and usage data

As you use the app, we store:

  • Which words you've viewed, marked "Mastered," or flagged as a "Tough Nut"
  • Your daily study streak and daily mastered-word count
  • Quiz/test history (scores, mode, date) where applicable
  • Your current position in the learning path (so "Continue" resumes the right word/unit)

This data is stored locally on your device (via browser localStorage) for instant, offline-first access, and is synced to our database (hosted on Supabase) tied to your account so your progress isn't lost if you reinstall the app or switch devices.

Analytics (only if enabled)

If Google Analytics 4 is configured for a given build of the app, we log product events — such as which tab you opened, which word you viewed, or when you marked a word mastered — along with standard technical data GA4 collects automatically (approximate location derived from IP, device/browser type, and a randomly generated analytics identifier). We do not enable Google Signals or ad personalization, and we do not use this data for advertising. If analytics isn't configured for your build, none of this data is collected or sent anywhere.

Fonts

The app loads two typefaces (Playfair Display and Inter) from Google Fonts. Loading a font causes your browser to request the font file directly from Google's servers, which — like any web request — exposes your IP address to Google for that request. We do not receive this data ourselves.

What we do not collect

  • No camera, microphone, contacts, or precise location access
  • No payment or financial information (InstaGRE is free)
  • No advertising identifiers, and no data is used for ads or ad targeting
  • No data is sold or shared with data brokers

2. How we use your information

  • To create and authenticate your account and keep you signed in
  • To save and sync your study progress across sessions and devices
  • To operate core features: streaks, learning-path unlocks, "Continue where you left off"
  • To understand aggregate feature usage and fix bugs (only where analytics is enabled)
  • To respond to support requests you send us

3. Who we share data with

We use a small number of infrastructure providers strictly as data processors — they don't get to use your data for their own purposes:

ProviderPurpose
SupabaseHosts our database and authentication functions (account records, progress, streaks, test history)
Google Analytics (GA4)Aggregate, privacy-respecting product analytics (only if enabled for a build)
Google FontsServes the typefaces used in the app's UI
VercelHosts the web app and its static assets

We do not otherwise sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties, and we don't use it for cross-app or cross-site advertising.

4. Data retention

We retain your account and progress data for as long as your account exists, so the app can keep working the way you expect between sessions. You can delete everything at any time — see below.

5. Deleting your account and data

You can delete your account and all associated data at any time, either instantly in-app or by email if you've lost access. Full steps and exactly what gets deleted: see our Delete Account page.

Note: word-content data (the dictionary itself) contains no personal information and isn't affected by account deletion.

6. Security

Your date of birth (used as your account password) is hashed with bcrypt before it's stored — we never store or transmit it in plain text after signup. All traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (HTTPS). Access to production data is restricted to the developer operating this app.

7. Children's privacy

InstaGRE is intended for GRE test-preparation, i.e. adults and older students, and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us at the email below and we will delete it.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data (editable directly in Profile → Edit Profile for your name)
  • Delete your data (see Section 5)
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format

To exercise any of these rights, email us at aivisionlabs555@gmail.com.

9. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we'll update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy.

10. Contact us
